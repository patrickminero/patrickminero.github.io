Memories of my vacation year travelling, voluntering and relocating to another country on a strict financial plan.

Travel booking tips

Travel agency or online? What websites to use? Should I have a set route or go with the flow? I asked myself these and many more questions for months before I started travelling and I always found mixed reviews.

Hotel, hostel or AirBnB?

Hostels are great for budget travelling especially in places like Europe or North America. In cheaper areas I splurged in a comfy bed and private bathroom. The pros and cons of hostels, hotels and AirBnB stays.

Safety advice!

Solo travelling safety tips. When travelling in diverse regions, you always have to keep an eye out, it’s common sense. Of course there were places where I thought I wasn’t safe.

Where to eat?

Can I afford that restaurant or should I go to the market? Is this really what locals eat or a tourist trap? When travelling for a long time budget is king and I always asked myself what everyday food is to the local community.

Places to drink

After walking all day with 40+ pounds on your back, a beer sounds magnificent. What if I can’t pay pub prices? Can I drink in the streets or in a park? What about craft beer, local wine or a fancy cocktail?

Best coffee in town

My favorite topic!!! As a former barista from the San Francisco Bay Area, I am a little spoiled on what coffee to drink. I always googled specialty coffee roasters in the area and made an effort on tasting as many as I could.
