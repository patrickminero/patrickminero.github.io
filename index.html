My Programming Journey

Back in the late 2000s, I studied Law in college then I decided to move to the California Bay Area where I would spend the next 11 years of my life.

After burning my self out in food-service management, working two jobs, I felt the need for a change in my lifestyle and wanted to improve my career options and hopefully have a better life quality.

After weeks of research, I set up a plan, worked extra hours, saved as much money as I could, and embarked on an open-ended trip around 4 continents and 25 countries volunteering in small family farms and businesses.

Eighteen months later and back in the routine, I moved to Barcelona, Spain, and while waiting to get my paperwork through local authorities, I started to learn web development.

I started my research and out of the thousands of options, I came across Codecademy. I decided to try out their “How to build websites with HTML, CSS, and Github Pages” skill path since it was free and wanted to make sure I liked computer programming as a career choice.

A few weeks later I was hooked, it was easier than what I expected it to be, very clear and straight forward but captivating enough to spark my curiosity.

When I finished the skill path I decided to upgrade to Codecademy PRO to learn more complex skills, I also researched popular coding tools and resources to start creating small projects and web pages.

After a few months of practice I felt like Codecademy, online resources and tutorials were not enough, I wanted real job experiences so I enrolled at Le Wagon were I learned technical and personals skills that developers use everyday.

Lanaguages, frameworks and resources

I started creating static websites withHTML and CSS to later move into using Bootstrap and Materialize for front end design and UI.

I spent a few months learning vanilla Javascript and once I felt confident I started to create projects with frameworks like Jquery and Vue.

I recently started to create full stack applications using MongoDB, Express, React and NodeJS.

At Le Wagon I learned and created fullstack applications using Ruby, Rails and PostgreSQL.

I also have experience with tools like Git, Figma, Gsap, Heroku and Cloudinary.
