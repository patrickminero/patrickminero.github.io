About me

Back in the late 2000s, I studied Law in college but never practiced, I was a year short of graduating when I decided to move to the California Bay Area where I would spend the next 11 years of my life. After burning my self out in food-service management, working two jobs to pay the bills, I felt the need for a change in my lifestyle and wanted to improve my career options and hopefully have a better life quality. After weeks of research, I set up a plan, worked extra hours, saved as much money as I could, and a year later I embarked on an open-ended trip around 4 continents and 25 countries volunteering in small family farms and businesses. It changed my life and helped me realize that change is possible when you set your mind to it.

Eighteen months later and back in the routine, I moved to Barcelona, Spain, and while I was waiting to get my paperwork through local authorities, I started to learn how to web development. I started my research and out of the thousands of options, I came across Codecademy. I decided to try out their “How to build websites with HTML, CSS, and Github Pages” skill path since it was free and wanted to make sure I liked computer programming as a career choice.

A few weeks later I was hooked, it was easier than what I expected it to be, very clear and straight forward but captivating enough to spark my curiosity. When I finished the skill path I decided to upgrade to Codecademy PRO to learn more complex skills, I also researched popular coding tools and resources to start creating small projects and web pages.

Almost a year has gone by since I started my web development journey, and I could not be happier about all the knowledge and experience I have acquired since then.
